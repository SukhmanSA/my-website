Introduction

Under the shadow of the Second World War, Germany had waged a genocidal war, which resulted in the mass murder of selected groups of innocent civilians of Europe. The number of people killed included approx 6 million Jews 200,000 Gypsies 1 million Polish civilians and 70,000 Germans who were considered physicaly or mentally unfit besides innumerable political opponents. The Nuremberg Tribunal scentenced only eleven leading Nazis to death. Many others were imprisoned for life. The retribution did come, yet the punishment was far short of the brutality and extent of crimes as the Allies did not wanted to be harsh on defeated Germany like the First World War as the harsh treatment of Germany after the First World War was considered as one of the causes of the Second World War.
