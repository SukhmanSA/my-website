The Birth of Weimar Republic

Germany, a powerful empire in the early years of twentieth century, fought First World War (1914-1918) alongside the Austrian and Ottoman empire against the Allies(England,France and Russia).All joined the war enthusiastically hoping to gain a quick victory. Little did they realised that the war would stretch on, draining Europe of its resources. Germany made initial gains by occupying France and Belgium. However the Allies, strengthened by US entry in 1917, won,defeating Germany and the Central powers in November 1918.

The defeat of Imperial Germany and the abdication of the emperor gave an opportuniy to parliament parties to recast German polity. A National Assembly met at Weimar and established a democratic constitution with a federal system.Deputies were now elected to the German parliament, on the basis of votes casted by all adults including women.

The Treaty of Versailles

On June 28, 1919, the Treaty of Versailles was signed at the Palace of Versailles outside Paris, France. Germany was the major aggressor in the First World War and had to pay reperations for the war. Germany lost its overseas colonies ,a tenth of its population, 13% of its territories, 75% of its iron and 26% of its coal to France, Poland, Denmark and Lithuania. The Allied Powers demilitarised Germany to weaken its power. Germany was forced to 6 million pounds compensation. Germans held the new Weimar Republic responsible for not only the defeat in war but the disgrace at Versailles.

Effects of the First World War

The First World War had following effects on Germany-

1. Unfortunately, the Weimar Republic was being made to pay for the sins of the old empire. The republic carried the war guilt and national humilation and was financially crippled by being forced to pay compensation.

2. Those who supported the Weimar Republic, mainly Socialists, Catholics and Democrats, became easy targets of attack in conservative nationalist circles. They were mockingly called the 'November Criminals'.

3. The soldiers came to be placed above civilians socialy. The media glorified the trench life while the life of soldiers at the trenches were so miserable.

4. The communists also started revolting against the Weimar Republic as they wanted soviet style governance.
